Community Service

Spring 2026

This spring, I volunteered at the local YMCA in Ithaca. I volunteer for the Adaptive and Inclusive program. In this program, counselors work with neurotypical and neurodivergent kids and help them learn to interact with each other in many different scenarios. Their favorite thing to do is to swim on fridays. I have a lot of fun working with these kids!

Fall 2025

This fall, I volunteered at the local YMCA in Ithaca. I volunteer for the Adaptive and Inclusive program. In this program, counselors work with children with mental disabilities and help them learn to interact with other children to make friends. I have a lot of fun working with these kids!

Spring 2025

I did my Service Saturday this semester at the IC food pantry. We removed all the food from the storage closet. Then, we wrote all of the expiration dates on the packages so that they are more visible. Finally, we put the goods back in the closet, reorganizing it in the process. It was a great experience because of how accesible it was to help. IC food pantry is located in CC, so it will be so easy to come back and help whenever I would like!

Fall 2024

On Saturday, November 2nd, I attended a volunteer day at Cayuga Nature Center. The Cayuga Nature Center provides indoor and outdoor education programs. Visitors are encouraged to explore and understand the natural history of the Cayuga Lake Basin! As a team, we were tasked to help declutter many surrounding flower beds and animal cages. They became overgrown from lack of available care. We also helped clean up the community herb garden. It was very rewarding to see all of the progress we made. I learned how to identify certain unwanted plants like chickery and burdock, the former which is really hard to get out of your hair! Because the Cayuga Nature Center is a space that supports educational programming, my work helped support the maintenance of Ithaca's natural lands, resources, and beauty. If offered, I would love to return to help clean up again. I will also be returning to the nature center in my spare time to play in their 6-story treehouse.
